Root Vegetable Dish
Batata mash
Kiribati

Batata Mash is a simple yet comforting Kiribati dish made from boiled sweet potatoes that are mashed until smooth. Often enriched with fresh coconut cream and a touch of salt, it carries a naturally sweet and creamy flavor. Served as a side dish or on its own, it is a humble but nourishing staple in many island households.

Soup
Teinai
Kiribati

Teinai is a traditional Kiribati pumpkin soup simmered with fresh coconut milk for a rich, velvety texture. Naturally sweet pumpkin blends with the creaminess of coconut, often seasoned lightly with herbs or spices. It is a warm and hearty dish that highlights the island’s reliance on local produce and coconut flavors.

Leaf-Wrapped Dish
Palusami
Kiribati

Palusami is a beloved Kiribati delicacy made by wrapping coconut cream, onions, and sometimes fish or meat in tender taro leaves. The bundles are then baked or steamed until soft, creating a savory and creamy parcel full of tropical flavor. Often served during gatherings and celebrations, it is both nourishing and deeply traditional.

Snack
Breadfruit Chips
Kiribati

Breadfruit Chips are a popular Kiribati snack made by slicing ripe breadfruit thinly and frying until golden and crisp. Lightly salted, they have a crunchy texture and a nutty, potato-like taste that makes them highly addictive. Enjoyed as a street snack or a side dish, they are a perfect way to savor the island’s versatile breadfruit.
