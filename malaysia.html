Nasi Lemak
Malaysia

Nasi Lemak is Malaysia’s national dish, a fragrant rice meal cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaves. Traditionally served with sambal (spicy chili paste), fried crispy anchovies, toasted peanuts, boiled egg, and cucumber slices, it’s a perfect balance of creamy, spicy, salty, and savory flavors. Often enjoyed for breakfast, but loved at any time of the day.

Char Kuey Teow
Malaysia

Char Kway Teow is a beloved Malaysian street food, featuring flat rice noodles stir-fried over high heat for a smoky “wok hei” flavor. Cooked with prawns, cockles, Chinese sausage, egg, bean sprouts, and chives, this dish is rich, savory, and slightly charred — a true hawker classic.

Flatbread
Roti Canai
Malaysia

Roti Canai is Malaysia’s famous flatbread, flaky on the outside and soft on the inside. Made by flipping and stretching dough before pan-frying, it is usually served with dhal curry or chicken curry, offering a comforting balance of crispy and fluffy textures.

Snack
Satay
Malaysia

Satay is a popular Malaysian street snack made of marinated meat skewers grilled over charcoal. Served with peanut sauce, ketupat (rice cakes), cucumber, and onions, it delivers a smoky, sweet, and savory taste that’s perfect for sharing.
